Julian Alexander's top 3 artists, by genre.

Julian has spent a stupid amount of time listening to music, and as such, has a solidified opinion on his top 3 genres, and their respective best/most influential artists. He has categorized and attempted to explain his reasoning below.

Techno (Detroit)

  1. Frankie Knuckles

    2. Frankie Knuckles is perhaps the proginator of not just the subtype of Techno, but house music as well. It's hard to distinctly draw the line, but Knuckles storied career has been one of the most influential you could find. Djing everywhere from New York, to Detroit, and rubbing shoulders with virtually the entire industry, he has been nothing short of prolific.

  2. Robert Hood/Floorplan

    3. Robert Hood is another early Dj, who has truly had an extremely eclectic mix of influences and music. Starting with "pure" techno, he has since adopted the moniker of Floorplan which is his own combination of old school gospel with electronica.

  3. Larry Heard

    4. Larry Heard is third on this list only because of his less prolific production catalogue, but his inventivesness in the genre can't be denied. Even though he is nearing 30+ years of music production, he is still going strong, with his Outer Acid Ep last year still being critically acclaimed, and a loyal fan base behind him.

Experimental/??????

  1. Aphex Twin

    2. Aphex Twin is perhaps the biggest "underground" celebrity in the musical community. Everyone from Frank Ocean, to Kanye, to Bjork and Radiohead are humongous fans of this shy and strange artist. A master of confusing his audience, Aphex Twin's music resists genre labels, and instead can fall anywhere in between ambient all the way to noise and EDM. Less of a genre definer, and more of a stereotype busting canister bomb, he will always be at the/or near the top of any list about experimental music.

  2. Death Grips

    3. Death Grips has earned an almost horrifying mainstream media appraisal, and that is most likely exactly what they are after. The music made by this group is undeniably violent and aggressive, but upon closer inspection Death Grips is undeniably sophisticated. Everything from their audio mixing, to the complexity of the beats and melodies, references the fact that Hill, Reid and Morrin are incredibly talented musicians.

  3. Arca

    4. Arca is an incredibly hard to define artist, even more so than the amorphous priorly mentioned artists. Arca creates music that is at once textural, atmospheric, and horrifying all at the same time. Resisting a niche, Arca has released music with him singing, silent, beat driven, and pop-y. Another intensely experimental and unique artist, that is only getting started.

RNB

  1. D'Angelo

    2. D'Angelo is the kind of frustrating artist that is so obssesive, so passionate, and so methodical, that he only releases music every decade or so. His entire catalogue only holds roughly 3 albums, with a career spanning over 18 years. However, he holds several accolades that just simply can't be replicated. His sophomore album, Voodoo, is quite simply, probably the best RNB album ever created, and his other works are less definitive, but no less good for that. A true master.

  2. James Brown

    3. James Brown rarely needs that much of an intro. He is known throughout the world as one of the most important figures in music, full stop. But I will make the argument that he belongs more in the RNB category than anything else. His sophisticated melodic arrangement mixes effortlessly with a voice that is shockingly sensual and exciting at the same time.

  3. Marvin Gaye

    4. Another legend, that doesn't really need an explanation. However I will say that I think his most underrated album is his Trouble Man soundtrack. Perfectly orchestrated, wonderfully arranged and simply atmospheric as hell.

It is important to keep in mind that the author (while being unusually obsessed with music) is still just a 22 year old baby, and his opinion should not be taken as gospel.
